Project DANA

Digital Archive & Narrative Assistant

A Triple-State Stealth Application designed to solve the legal paradox of domestic abuse: victims need to document evidence to win in court, but the act of documenting is inherently dangerous.

2. Proving "Course of Conduct"

In criminal law (especially Scottish Domestic Abuse Act), a single argument isn't a crime. A pattern of behavior is.

"The data model is designed to visualize escalation. Instead of scattered notes, DANA presents a timeline of increasing frequency, crucial for prosecution."

3. Family Court Evidence

Civil courts focus on the Impact on the Child. Vague claims of "bad parenting" fail. Specific, quantified logs of regressive behavior succeed.

"Features like the 'Child Quotes' logger and 'Post-Contact Checklist' create the granular data needed for Scott Schedules and custody hearings."

4. The Empowerment Workflow

Survivors often struggle to name their abuse due to gaslighting. DANA uses AI to suggest legal classifications (e.g., translating "He took my cards" to "Financial Abuse"), but grants the user final editorial control via the "Review" dropdown.
