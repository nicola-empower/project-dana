The Core Problem: A Legal Paradox

Survivors must prove a "Course of Conduct" (a pattern of abuse) and "Impact on the Child" (for family court), but the very act of documenting this evidence is dangerous. Project DANA solves this by existing as a **Dual-State Application**.

Data Insight: Tracking "Course of Conduct" (Criminal Law)

A key piece of evidence is proving a *pattern* of abuse. The app tracks incident frequency, allowing survivors and legal teams to visualize escalation over time.

Data Insight: Child Welfare Impact (Family Law)

In family court, the focus is the child's welfare. The "Post-Contact Checklist" gathers objective data on the child's state after returning from contact.

Key UX & Safety-Driven Features

The UI/UX is not just about aesthetics; it's a core part of the safety mechanism, designed to reduce cognitive load on a user in distress.

Cognitive Load Reduction

A calming, high-contrast palette and large, tap-friendly toggle buttons (instead of small checkboxes) assist users who may be shaking or in a state of hyper-arousal.

Historical Backdating

Survivors often find help *after* a crisis. The app allows robust logging of past incidents (Date, Time, Location) to build a complete timeline immediately.

Dual Legal Export

The data structure is designed to be exported for two different legal systems: "Course of Conduct" for criminal cases and "Child Impact" logs for civil/family court.
